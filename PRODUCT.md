# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Self-hosters and homelabbers who run their own infrastructure (Immich, Jellyfin, Nextcloud, Home Assistant, and the like). They arrive from a search at the exact moment before an upgrade, asking one anxious question: "is it safe to update?" They are technical, skim-reading, and skeptical of marketing. Job to be done: get a fast, trustworthy, sourced answer on whether a release is safe to apply, and learn what would break, without reading the whole changelog.

## Product Purpose

Bumplog tracks releases of popular self-hosted apps and answers, per app, whether the latest version is safe to update, with breaking changes called out and every verdict traceable to its GitHub release. Success is the returning maintenance ritual: a self-hoster who bookmarks the page and rechecks before each upgrade cycle, trusting the verdict enough to act on it.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, precise, calm. The quiet authority you check before you upgrade: confident without drama, sourced rather than asserted. The voice is plainspoken and technical; it respects the reader's expertise and never oversells. Closest reference is Linear: precise, restrained, calm density, first-class dark mode.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS: card grids, soft status pills, purple-to-blue gradients, identical icon-plus-heading-plus-text blocks.
- Warm cream, sand, or beige "editorial paper" backgrounds. The warm-tinted near-white body is the saturated AI default; the background must be a true or cool neutral, or the brand's own hue, never warmth by default.
- Loud, flashy, over-designed: neon, glassmorphism, decoration without purpose, animation for its own sake.
- Heavy enterprise dashboard chrome.

## Design Principles

- Source of truth, visible. Every verdict traces to a GitHub release; provenance is a feature, shown rather than claimed.
- Answer first, calmly. The safety call is the hero: legible in one glance, stated in plain language, never hedged into noise.
- Restraint is trust. Density and precision over decoration; one accent, hairline structure, generous rhythm. An element that does not earn its place gets cut.
- Respect the expert. Skim-friendly, technical, no marketing fluff. The reader knows their own stack better than we do.
- Quiet by default, legible always. Calm surfaces, but contrast and hierarchy are never traded away for elegance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum: body text at least 4.5:1, large or bold text at least 3:1, status never carried by color alone (always paired with a label or shape). Full keyboard reachability with a visible focus ring. Honor prefers-reduced-motion and prefers-color-scheme, with first-class light and dark themes. Tabular numerals for versions and dates.
